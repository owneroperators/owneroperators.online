What goes in the truck — everything but the recording gear (that's [the rig](/retreat/rig/)).

## Clothing (mountain June — warm days, cold nights)

- [ ] Layers — the nights drop into the 40s–50s
- [ ] Rain shell (afternoon mountain storms)
- [ ] Swimsuits + towels (lake + sauna)
- [ ] Sturdy shoes for the backcountry
- [ ] Sun hat + sunglasses (high-elevation sun)

## Water + fun gear (Lake Cascade is right there)

- [ ] **Paddleboard** ×1 · **inflatable kayaks** (single + double) · **fly rod** ×1
- [ ] **Pump** for the inflatables (+ a hand-pump backup) — nobody's lung-inflating a kayak at 5,000 ft
- [ ] Paddles — SUP + kayak (1 + at least 2) · SUP leash · fins / fin needles
- [ ] **PFDs for everyone on the water** — Idaho law, one per person aboard
- [ ] **Repair kit** + patch glue — one thorn ends the trip otherwise
- [ ] Dry bag for phones/keys · a towel pile
- [ ] **Idaho fishing license** — grab it online before you go · flies / tippet / nippers / net

> Buttercup Boat Launch is a short walk from the house — park at the house and walk gear down if the ramp lot is packed.

## Kids

- [ ] Lake gear: life jackets (sized per kid), water shoes, sand toys
- [ ] Rainy-afternoon backup: games, cards, movies downloaded offline
- [ ] Snacks for the drive + meltdown insurance

## Household / kitchen

- [ ] Big grocery haul — **shop before the hill**
- [ ] Coffee + a good grinder/maker if the house one is weak
- [ ] Cooler for the lake / drive
- [ ] Trash bags, dish soap, paper towels (9 people burns through it)
- [ ] Travel mugs / water bottles

## Health / safety

- [ ] Sunscreen + bug spray (lakeside = mosquitoes at dusk)
- [ ] First-aid kit + meds — nearest real pharmacy is McCall
- [ ] **Eye masks** — long June daylight, no blinds reported
- [ ] Headlamp / flashlight
