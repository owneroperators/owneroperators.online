# - LOSS LEADER LP -

_A high-voltage, glitch-soaked album about nostalgia, scams, and survival - rock music for anyone who's ever doomscrolled heartbreak and still ended up laughing, shouting, and dancing like the floor is collapsing (*it is*)._

# Liner Notes:

## The Long Con:

A song about systems so vast we mistake them for weather. It's about trying to stay honest in a world designed to reward performance.

## Totally Fine:

Suburbia in 4/4 time. A portrait of comfort that quietly erodes you-if you're not paying attention.

## Post-Truth Light:

Part satire, part sermon. Inspired by late-stage capitalism, government software, and the creeping suspicion that irony might've finally won.

## Deadpan Clean:

Absurdism as medicine. Gallows humor as a survival strategy. This one came from doomscrolling with the volume off and a laugh track stuck in my head.

## Heart With Handles:

An emotional tech manual disguised as a relationship postmortem. Love, rendered in code and static. Every verse tries to say "I miss you" without using those words.

## More Than Enough:

Wires, glitches, and imperfect connections. A love song inspired by that strange sensation of feeling most human when the systems break down.

## Digital Love Glitch:

Equal parts joke and prayer. This track started as an attempt to write the worst song possible-and accidentally became one of my favorites. A post-punk elegy for broken machines trying to feel something real.

## Echoes and Static:

Nostalgia is a feedback loop. This one began as a journal entry and ended up as a goodbye letter to a version of myself that still believed time moved in a straight line.

## Highway Shadows:

Motion as memory. Built on repetition-both lyrically and structurally. It's about the in-between places: the long drive, the liminal rest stops, the moment after something ends but before you admit it did. Feels like cruising through a dream you forgot you had.

## Caught In The Chaos:

A quiet anthem for rebuilding with what's left. About choosing to care even when nothing's guaranteed. Ends where it begins: not with answers, but with momentum.

## Breaker Breaker:

The title is truckers' slang for "I need to talk" - cutting in on the channel, asking if anyone's listening. The A7(b9)/Eb in the bridge is a tritone substitution that shouldn't resolve - it doesn't. Everything past that point is an outro that keeps finding new ways to not arrive.
