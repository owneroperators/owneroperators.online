## Leads & flourishes (present in most songs — handle with care)

A lead is **exposed** — you can't hide a bad one under chorus the way you can a pad. So:

- **Draw leads in** (or play slow) for precision. A memorable lead played perfectly beats a busy one played shaky.
- **Keep them singable and simple.** The best synth leads are hooks you could hum — not displays of speed. (Same note as the lyrics: concrete and memorable over busy.)
- **Use portamento / glide** (set the lead patch to mono + glide) for that liquid synth-lead slide between notes — expression without finger dexterity.
- **Double the lead** in octaves, or with a guitar line, to make it thick and intentional.
- **Pitch-bend and mod wheel** for vibrato and dives — character a keyboardist's hands would give you, faked with a strip.

For **flourishes / ear candy**:

- **Arpeggiator** bursts, delay throws (automate the send up for one note), quick filter sweeps, little drawn-in fills between vocal phrases.
- These are best **drawn in** — flourishes are where precision reads as polish.

## Tying it to the aesthetic

The record's described as synths "flickering between analog warmth and software bug." The **warmth** is the saw pad + chorus above. The **bug/glitch** is the other half — g[truncated at screen edge] flourish. Warmth on the pads, malfunction on the flourishes = the whole OWNER/OPERATORS synth identity in one sentence.
